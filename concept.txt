No, they don't overlap. Think of them in layers:

Core concept: Rubik's Cube = life's chaos and complexity before going Out of Office.
Interaction: The cube slowly solves itself as users scroll through the site.
Progress system: Each section completed solves one face of the cube.
Visual transitions: The solved colors can transition into beach/ocean colors.
Easter eggs: Hidden interactions tied to the cube.

You can absolutely combine them.

Suggested flow:

Landing page → Scrambled cube floating.
Scroll through sections → Cube gradually solves.
Final section → Fully solved cube + "Out of Office Activated."
Hidden interactions:
Click cube multiple times → Auto-reply generator.
Leave page idle → Cube sleeps.
Konami code or secret clicks → Lagos stress stats.
Drag cube around → It reacts or scrambles briefly.

So it becomes:

Chaos (Lagos) → Journey → Reset (Out of Office)

The Rubik's Cube is simply the visual metaphor carrying the user through that journey, while the Easter eggs reinforce the playful, community-driven, gamified vibe that fits your style.
